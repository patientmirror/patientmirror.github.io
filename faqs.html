Patient MIRROR

Frequently Asked Questions

General

What is the Patient MIRROR study?

Patient MIRROR (Medical Instructional Resource Review Of Representation) is a research project that systematically examines the demographic characteristics of patients depicted in clinical vignettes used in medical education. The study aims to characterize patterns of representation across dimensions such as age, sex, race, and ethnicity.

Who is conducting this research?

The project is being led by Dr Marta Rowh, Assistant Professor of Emergency Medicine, Emory University School of Medicine.

What types of clinical vignettes are included in the analysis?

USMLE-style questions, textbook cases, standardized patient scenarios, etc.

What demographic dimensions does the study examine?

Age, sex assigned at birth, race, ethnicity, socioeconomic status, etc.

How can I contribute my dataset or curricular materials?

Study materials and annotated datasets can be uploaded via Dropbox. Please see the link on the homepage. You may contribute your curricular material anonymously, if you prefer.

Will my curricular materials be safe?

The contributed curricular materials will only be used for research, after de-identification. Your curricular material will not be shared with non-project personnel.
